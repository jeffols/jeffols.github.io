Thinking

Ontology as architecture

Systems inherit the shape of the concepts they model. Get the ontology right and the code writes itself. Get it wrong and no amount of engineering saves you.

Context over instruction

The best systems don't need more rules — they need better context. Engineer the information environment and let intelligence emerge from structure.

Simplicity is the residue of understanding

Complexity isn't a problem to manage — it's a symptom of incomplete comprehension. Simplify by understanding more deeply, not by hiding more aggressively.
